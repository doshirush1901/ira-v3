## Project Overview
Machinecraft designed and built India's first locally manufactured IMG (In-Mold Graining)
thermoforming machine. IMG is the process used to create soft-touch, textured interior
panels for cars — instrument panels, door trims, center consoles, armrests. Previously,
these machines were only available from European manufacturers like FRIMO (Germany) at
significantly higher cost.

## IMG Process Explained
In-Mold Graining (IMG) is a thermoforming process where a pre-printed or textured TPO
(thermoplastic olefin) film is vacuum-formed onto a textured nickel-shell mold. The result
is a soft-touch, grained surface that looks and feels like leather — used in premium car
interiors. The grain is transferred from the mold to the film with perfect fidelity.

## Background: YFG Prototype Project
Before the production machine, Machinecraft collaborated with FRIMO Germany on a
feasibility study for Maruti Suzuki's YFG SUV interior project:
- OEM: Maruti Suzuki + Toyota (Urban Cruiser type SUV)
- Part: Instrument panel mid-panels (soft-touch, grained)
- Volume: 160,000 car sets/year, 5-year program (~800K parts)
- Partners: IAC (Tier-1), FRIMO (Germany), Kolon Korea (TPO foil)
- Outcome: 100 sample parts produced; proved the concept; led to production order
