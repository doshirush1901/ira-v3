**Heater System:**
- Top Heater: 336 IR Ceramic elements (14 x 24 grid), 500W each, SSR controlled by PLC
- Bottom Heater: 336 IR Ceramic elements (14 x 24 grid), 300W each, SSR controlled by PLC
- 8 temperature control zones per heater
- Bottom heater moves up/down via servo motor to follow HDPE sheet sag (250-400mm allowance)
- Sag sensor on bottom heater detects sheet sag level
- IR Pyrometer for real-time sheet temperature measurement

**Special Design Feature — Bottom Heater Sag-Following System:**
Machinecraft designed a special open-type PF1 (no chamber) where the bottom heater moves up/down as per the sheet sag distance to optimally heat the HDPE sheet. As the HDPE sags during heating, sensors detect the sag level and the bottom heater moves downward using a servo motor mechanism to maintain optimal heating distance. This was the "New Concept" referenced in the quote.

**Sheet Loading:** Automatic sheet loader (servo motor driven up/down + in/out)
**Sheet Clamping:** Electric servo motor driven (rack & pinion)
**Mould Table Movement:** Electric servo motor driven (accelerate/decelerate for soft touch)
**Plug Assist:** Electric servo motor driven (top table)
**Cooling:** 12 x EBM Papst centrifugal fans (26 m3/hr each) with IR sensor closed-loop control
**Control:** Delta PLC + Delta 15" HMI touchscreen
**Safety:** Light guard (Sick/PnF), emergency switches, interlocks

## 7. CASE STUDY NARRATIVE ELEMENTS

### The Challenge
ALP Group, a $200M automotive components conglomerate and market leader in weather-stripping for Japanese OEMs in India, needed to enter the **pickup truck bedliner** market for Mahindra & Mahindra. The application required deep-draw thermoforming of 4mm thick HDPE sheets into large, complex bedliner geometries — a technically demanding process requiring:
- Large forming area to accommodate full-size truck bed dimensions
- Deep draw capability (500mm+) for the bedliner's contoured shape
- Robust pneumatic/mechanical systems for thick HDPE processing
- Automated sheet loading for production efficiency
- Imported tooling integration

### The Solution
Machinecraft designed and built a **custom deep-draw automatic thermoforming machine** specifically for this application, featuring:
- Sheet auto-loader frame for automated production
- Bottom heater trolley with movable design
- Mould upward movement mechanism for deep-draw forming
- Heavy-duty 2800mm pneumatic cylinders
- PLC control system (by Think Engineering)
- Capability to process 4mm thick HDPE sheets

### The Broader Relationship
The bedliner machine was the anchor of a deepening relationship:
- **Consulting engagement:** Rushabh provided monthly consulting visits to ALP for thermoforming business development
- **FRIMO connection:** Machinecraft introduced ALP to FRIMO (Germany) for LFI/SSRIM/WCM line technology — thermoforming + PU for EVs, agriculture, buses, trains
- **Multiple machines:** ALP went on to order additional machines (mat machine, potentially PF1-1525/PF1-2515 PRO)
- **Ongoing support:** Regular site visits, spare parts supply, programming updates, safety improvements

### The Outcome
- Machine producing bedliners for **Mahindra & Mahindra** — ALP's sole customer for this product
- ALP expanded thermoforming capabilities with additional machine orders
- Machinecraft established itself as ALP's thermoforming technology partner
- Relationship expanded from machine supply to consulting and technology introduction

### What Makes This Project Unique
1. **Custom machine** — not a standard catalog model, designed specifically for HDPE bedliner deep-draw
2. **Automotive OEM supply chain** — producing for Mahindra & Mahindra, one of India's largest automakers
3. **Full ecosystem discussion** — thermoforming machine + trimming + raw material + tooling (even though only machine was supplied)
4. **Gateway to larger relationship** — led to consulting, FRIMO introduction, additional machine orders
5. **$200M group** — large, multi-entity industrial conglomerate as customer
