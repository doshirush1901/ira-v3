# Dutch Tides — Hydroponic Ebb-Flow Trays

## Customer
- **Name:** Dutch Tides
- **Location:** Bergschenhoek, Netherlands
- **Industry:** Agriculture / Hydroponics / Controlled Environment Agriculture
- **Contact:** Jaap (owner)

## Project Overview
Dutch Tides needed the largest thermoforming machine in Europe to produce massive hydroponic
ebb-flow trays. These trays are used in commercial greenhouse operations for growing plants
without soil — water and nutrients flow across the tray surface in controlled cycles.

## Machine
- **Model:** PF1-X-6520 (custom XL)
- **Forming Area:** 6500 x 2000 mm (6.5 x 2 meters) — largest thermoformer in Europe
- **Atlas Project No:** 24006
- **Status:** Installed at Dutch Tides facility in Bergschenhoek

## Technical Details
- **Material:** 4mm PS (Polystyrene)
- **Cycle Time:** ~150 seconds per cycle
- **Application:** Hydroponic ebb-flow trays for commercial greenhouse operations
- **Special:** Machinecraft supplied both the machine AND the sheets, then spent two weeks
  on-site fine-tuning the process with the Dutch Tides team

## Story Angle
This is a "largest in Europe" story. An Indian company (Machinecraft) built and installed
the biggest thermoforming machine on European soil — in the Netherlands, a country known
for its advanced greenhouse/agriculture technology. The machine produces trays for the
booming controlled environment agriculture (CEA) industry.

## Key Proof Points
