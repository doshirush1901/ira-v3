# ALP Group / ALP Aeroflex — Mahindra Scorpio Pickup Truck Bedliner

**Industry:** automotive | **Country:** India | **Year:** 2022
**Machine:** PF1-3520/S/A (Custom) | **Material:** HDPE, 5.5mm

---

## The Challenge

ALP Group, a $200M automotive components conglomerate supplying Japanese OEMs in India, needed to enter the pickup truck bedliner market for Mahindra & Mahindra. The application demanded deep-draw thermoforming of 5.5mm thick HDPE sheets into large, complex bedliner geometries (mould size 1830 x 2550 mm) — a technically demanding process requiring precise sag control, automated sheet loading, and integration with imported tooling from Thailand.

## The Solution

Machinecraft designed and built a custom PF1-3520/S/A — a special open-type PF1 series with a 3500 x 2000 mm forming area and an innovative sag-following bottom heater system. As the HDPE sheet sags during heating (250-400mm), sensors on the bottom heater detect the sag level and servo motors move the heater downward to maintain optimal heating distance. The machine includes automatic sheet loading, 560 IR ceramic heating elements (top + bottom), servo-driven mould table (2.2 tonne capacity), plug assist, and closed-loop cooling with IR pyrometer feedback.

## Technical Highlights

- 3500 x 2000 mm forming area — one of the largest single-station machines built for bedliner production
- Innovative sag-following bottom heater: servo-driven up/down mechanism tracks HDPE sheet sag in real-time using sensors
- 560 IR ceramic heating elements (280 top + 280 bottom) with 8-zone PLC control per heater bank
- 435 kW total connected load with 300 m³/hr Busch vacuum pump and 9000-litre vacuum tank
- Automatic sheet loader with servo-driven in/out and up/down mechanism for production efficiency
- Closed-loop cooling: 12 EBM Papst centrifugal fans with IR pyrometer auto-shutoff at set temperature
- Servo-driven mould table (2.2 tonnes), clamp frame, plug assist (450 kg), and heater movement — all-servo machine
- Custom designed from scratch — not a catalog model — specifically engineered for HDPE bedliner deep-draw

## The Outcome

The machine was installed at ALP Aeroflex's plant in Nashik (MIDC Satpur) and is producing bedliners for Mahindra Scorpio pickup trucks. ALP is M&M's sole supplier for this component. The project anchored a deepening relationship: Machinecraft now provides consulting services, introduced ALP to FRIMO (Germany) for LFI/SSRIM technology, and has supplied additional machines to the group.
