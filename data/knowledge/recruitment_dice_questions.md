# Recruitment DICE questions (short behavioural)

Used in Stage 2 recruitment emails. Pick 2–4 questions per role or use the default set for all.

## Default set (all roles)

1. Describe a time when you had to give difficult feedback to a colleague or a shop-floor worker. How did you approach it and what was the outcome?
2. When multiple stakeholders (e.g. procurement, production, customer) pull in different directions, how do you decide what to prioritise and how do you communicate that?
3. What do you do when you’re under pressure to meet a deadline and quality or safety would be compromised if you cut corners?
4. Describe a situation where you had to learn something new (a process, a system, or a technical detail) quickly to unblock work. How did you go about it?
5. What’s one thing you’re proud of in your career that had nothing to do with hitting a target — and why does it matter to you?

## Plant Manager / Production Planning

Same as default set; can add:
- How do you prefer to work with the Factory Head or senior production lead — do you lean on their experience, challenge them, or something else? Give a short example.

## CAD / Design Engineer

1. How do you handle last-minute drawing changes or revision requests when fabrication is already waiting?
2. Describe a time when you had to give difficult feedback to a colleague. How did you approach it?
3. When stakeholders (e.g. production, procurement) need different levels of detail from your drawings, how do you prioritise and communicate?
4. Describe a situation where you had to learn a new tool or standard quickly to unblock a release.

## PLC Programming

1. When you’re under pressure to meet a FAT date and an integration issue appears, how do you balance speed and quality?
2. Describe a time when you had to learn a new PLC or HMI platform quickly. How did you go about it?
3. How do you coordinate with the electrical and mechanical teams when there’s a conflict (e.g. I/O or mounting)?
4. What’s one thing you’re proud of in your career that wasn’t about hitting a target?

## Procurement

1. When a critical component is late and production is blocked, how do you prioritise and communicate with the Factory Head and Sales?
2. Describe a time when you had to give difficult feedback to a vendor or internal stakeholder. What was the outcome?
3. How do you decide when to chase the same vendor versus sourcing an alternate?
4. Describe a situation where you had to learn something new quickly (e.g. a new category or system) to unblock an order.

## CAM

1. How do you handle a last-minute program change when the machine is already set up?
2. Describe a time when you had to learn new CAM software or a new machine quickly. How did you go about it?
3. When production and design disagree on a machining approach, how do you decide what to prioritise?
4. What’s one thing you’re proud of in your career that wasn’t about hitting a target?
