# Plant Manager case study — Production planning (Machinecraft Umargam)

**Purpose:** Sent to Plant Manager candidates (e.g. Harshul Jain) to assess depth of production planning and ability to read, comprehend, and suggest a solution based on real Machinecraft process and data patterns.

## Case study: PF1 project — Delayed procurement and blocked fitting

**Project:** 22014 – ALP Delhi – PF1 3520 SA  
(PF1 thermoforming machine, 3.5 m × 2 m forming area.)  
**Customer:** ALP Delhi.  
**Committed Ex-Works Umargam dispatch date:** 6 weeks from today.

---

### Current state (as of this week)

**BOM**  
- Frozen. Mechanical BOM, cylinders & pneumatics, and electrical sections are signed off.

**Procurement**  
- Most raw material and hardware have been received.  
- One critical item is late: **Festo plug cylinders (DSBC-100-100-PPVA)**.  
  - Order (O:) was placed 5 weeks ago.  
  - Receive (R:) was expected 2 weeks ago.  
  - Vendor has communicated a further 2-week delay.  
  - No substitute has been ordered.

**Fabrication**  
- Main plates 1 & 2 are welded (W: done).  
- Base plate and front/back body frames are in progress.  
- Welding team will finish the remaining fabrication in about 1 week if no new delays.

**Fitting**  
- Fitting (FT:) for HMI box, cross cutter, vacuum tank, and **pneumatic cylinders** cannot start until the Festo cylinders are received.  
- Assembly and electrical cannot fully proceed without these.

**People**  
- **Factory Head** is aware and has asked for a “plan A and plan B” from production.  
- **Procurement** says they are chasing the vendor daily but have no firm date.  
- **Sales** has not yet informed the customer of a possible slip.

---

### Your task (candidate)

As the **Plant Manager for Umargam**, how would you approach this situation?

In **200–300 words**, outline:
1. What you would **prioritise**.
2. **Whom** you would involve (e.g. Procurement, Factory Head, CAD, Sales).
3. **Concrete steps** in the first 48 hours and in the first week.
4. If you would ask for any **additional information** before deciding, say what you’d need.

Project code 22014 and ALP Delhi are illustrative; Festo DSBC and gate patterns are consistent with real procurement/fabrication/fitting flow.
