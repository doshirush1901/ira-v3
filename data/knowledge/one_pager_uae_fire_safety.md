# One-Pager: UAE Fire-Safety Manufacturer

**Use for:** First contact, discovery call, or proposal cover with a UAE fire-safety / emergency-equipment manufacturer (e.g. fire fighting vehicles, alarm systems, suppression systems, safety equipment).

---

## 1. Machine recommendation

| Need | Recommendation |
|------|----------------|
| **Large fire-safety components** (covers, housings, panels) | **PF1-4525** (4500 × 2500 mm forming area) — same class as NAFFCO. |
| **Smaller parts or budget-conscious** | **PF1-X-3520** or **PF1-X-3020** — smaller footprint, still servo, suitable for many components. |
| **Very large / one of the largest in region** | **PF1-4525** — 500 kW, 952 heater elements (476 top + 476 bottom), 400 m³/hr vacuum; one of the largest thermoforming machines in the Middle East. |

**Key specs (PF1-4525):**
- Forming area: **4500 × 2500 mm**
- Total power: **500 kW** (heater)
- Heater: IR Ceramic (e.g. Elstein Germany) — 952 elements
- Vacuum: **400 m³/hr**
- Movements: **Electric servo**
- Sheet thickness: 2–12 mm (cut sheet); materials: ABS, HDPE, PP, PS, etc. as needed for fire-safety parts.

## 3. Typical cycle time

- **PF1-4525 / large fire-safety parts:** Cycle time typically **90–150 seconds** per part depending on sheet thickness, material, and draw depth. For 3–6 mm ABS/HDPE/PS components, expect **~90–120 seconds**; thicker or deeper parts toward **120–180 seconds**.
- **NAFFCO:** Case study does not publish exact cycle time; use “similar production rates to NAFFCO” and offer to validate on their part size and material once known.

---

## 4. What to say in the first meeting

**Opening (rapport + reference)**  
- “We’ve worked with fire-safety manufacturers in the Gulf — for example NAFFCO in Dubai. They run our PF1-4525 for large thermoformed components and have ordered more machines since. I’d like to understand your parts and volumes so we can see if the same class of machine fits.”

**Qualification (ask, don’t assume)**  
- **Application:** What exactly do you thermoform? (covers, housings, panels, other?)
- **Material & thickness:** What material and sheet thickness? (e.g. ABS, HDPE, 2–6 mm?)
- **Part size & depth:** Max sheet size and max draw depth?
- **Volume:** Parts per day/shift or per month?
- **Current pain:** Existing machine (capacity, quality, downtime) or first thermoforming line?

**Offer (machine + reference)**  
- “For large fire-safety components we typically recommend a machine in the same class as NAFFCO’s — our PF1-4525: 4.5 × 2.5 m forming area, 500 kW, full servo. Cycle times in the 90–150 second range depending on your part. We can send a one-pager and then a formal quote once we lock your part size and material.”

**Close**  
- “Next step: I’ll send you a short one-pager with machine recommendation, NAFFCO reference, and typical cycle times. If you can share one part drawing or dimensions, we’ll align the quote to your application. When’s a good time for a 20-minute technical call?”

---

## 5. Pricing (indicative, verify before sending)

- **PF1-4525 (UAE/Middle East):** ~**USD 200,000** EXW (reference: NAFFCO order). Confirm with Plutus or latest quote for current pricing and terms.
- **Payment terms (UAE):** Often 25% LC, 65% on inspection, 10% on commissioning (align to their procurement).

---

## 6. Do not

- Invent cycle time for NAFFCO’s exact parts — use “typical 90–150 seconds” and “we can validate for your part.”
- Quote without confirming part size, material, and thickness.
- Forget to mention repeat business (NAFFCO’s additional orders) as proof of reliability.
