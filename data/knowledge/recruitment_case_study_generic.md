# Generic recruitment case study — Process scenario (Machinecraft Umargam)

**Purpose:** Used for CAD, Design Engineer, PLC, Procurement, and CAM candidates when no role-specific case study exists. Assesses ability to read a short process scenario and suggest next steps.

---

## Scenario: Drawing release delay and fabrication hold

**Context:** Machinecraft builds thermoforming machines at Umargam. Orders flow from BOM freeze → procurement → fabrication (cutting, welding) → fitting → assembly → electrical → FAT → dispatch. CAD releases drawings; fabrication cannot start a phase without released drawings.

**Situation:**  
Project 22015 – PF1 3020 for a domestic customer. Committed dispatch: 8 weeks from today. Mechanical BOM is frozen. Most fabrication drawings have been released; the base plate and frame weldment pack (Drawings D-22015-04 to D-22015-07) was due for release last week. The CAD lead has been on leave; one drawing (D-22015-06) has a revision pending from the Factory Head’s review. Fabrication has finished the previous job and is waiting on this pack to start base plate and frames. Procurement has ordered raw material against the BOM; material is arriving. The customer has not been told of any risk to the date.

**Your task:**  
In **150–250 words**, outline what you would prioritise, whom you would involve (e.g. CAD, Factory Head, Production), and what concrete steps you would take in the next 48 hours and in the next week. If you would need more information before deciding, say what you’d need.
