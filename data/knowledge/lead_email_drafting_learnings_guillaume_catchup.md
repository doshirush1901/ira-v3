# Lead email drafting learnings (Guillaume / Plastochim catch-up)

**Source:** User feedback on draft to guillaume.faure@plastochim-thermoformage.com (March 2026). Apply to **next lead** and future catch-up/client emails.

---

## What to do

- **Show the right asset:** For clients/leads where we want to showcase new capability, use the **new video of the Italian lab machine with universal frames** (YouTube). Do not default to "SAM-PF1 Lab Edition" as the main offer unless the lead has asked for it.
- **No unsolicited price ranges in catch-ups:** Do not include "recent quotes for PF1 series have ranged from 32,000–36,000 EUR" (or similar) in catch-up emails. Only mention prices when the lead has asked for a quote or we are sending a formal quote.
- **Avoid odd phrasing:** Do not use "new packaging format" or similar vague tooling/packaging lines unless the lead has explicitly asked about formats or tooling. Keep tooling support offers concrete and brief.

---

## What to avoid

- **Do not push "SAM-PF1 Lab Edition"** unless the lead has expressed interest in a lab machine or R&D line.
- **Do not add recent quote price ranges** (e.g. "32–36k EUR") in warm catch-up emails.
- **Do not use "new packaging format" / "new packaging formats"** as a generic question—it reads weird; if asking about tooling, be specific or skip.
