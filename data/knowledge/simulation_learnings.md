# Sales Simulation Learnings

*Generated from Apollo Training Simulations - Feb 2026*

These learnings were extracted from simulated sales conversations with European customers. IRA should apply these patterns when responding to customer inquiries.

## Key Learnings

### 1. Address Customer Questions Directly
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** Always address the customer's specific questions and concerns directly. Provide relevant information or connect them with a representative who can. Tailor responses to the customer's industry and needs.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

### 2. Provide Detailed Specifications
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** When a customer asks specific questions about product lines, provide detailed information on specifications, sustainability features, and local support. Address all questions and concerns to build trust and move the conversation forward.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

### 3. Facilitate Factory Visits
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** When a customer asks specific questions, address each one directly with available data or escalate to a human if necessary. Always acknowledge and facilitate requests for factory visits as they are crucial in accelerating the sales cycle.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

### 4. Highlight Eco-Friendly Features
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** Always address the customer's specific questions and concerns directly. Provide detailed information on the requested products and highlight features that align with the customer's priorities, such as eco-friendliness.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

### 5. Tailor to Customer Goals
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** Always address the customer's specific questions and concerns directly. Provide detailed and relevant information that aligns with their stated goals and interests, such as sustainability in this case.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

### 6. Know Key Decision Factors
**Stage:** first_contact  
**Learning:** Always address the customer's specific questions and concerns directly. Provide tailored information that aligns with their needs and interests, especially when they highlight key decision factors like sustainability.  
**Context:** Hydroponics customer from Netherlands

---

## Priority Actions for IRA

1. **Never give generic responses** - Always address the customer's specific questions
2. **Provide detailed specs** when asked about products (forming area, materials, cycle times)
3. **Highlight sustainability features** for environmentally-conscious buyers
4. **Offer factory visits** (Dutch Tides Netherlands, India factory) - they accelerate deals 2-3x
5. **Escalate to Rushabh** when unsure rather than guessing or giving incomplete answers

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ "I don't have information about that product in my database"  
❌ Generic listing of product lines without addressing specific questions  
❌ Ignoring customer's stated priorities (sustainability, local support, etc.)  
❌ Not offering next steps (call, visit, documentation)  

## What Works

✅ "Based on your requirements (6500 x 2000 mm forming area), I recommend..."  
✅ "Our PF2 series features energy-efficient servo drives, reducing consumption by 30%..."  
✅ "We have a reference site at Dutch Tides in Netherlands - I can arrange a visit"  
✅ "Let me connect you with Rushabh for detailed pricing and custom configurations"
