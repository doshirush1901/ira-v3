**To:** harshuljain.0392@gmail.com

**Body:**

Dear Harshul,

Thank you for your interest in Machinecraft Technologies and for applying for the position of Plant Manager at our Umargam facility.

We have reviewed your application and would like to learn more about your profile in the context of **Machinecraft’s machinery building process** at Umargam (12-phase workflow from BOM and procurement through fabrication, assembly, testing, and dispatch). The following 10 questions are specific to how we run production and how the Plant Manager coordinates with procurement, CAD/CAM, and the Factory Head. Please answer so we can assess fit:

1. **BOM to procurement:** How would you coordinate the transition from BOM creation to procurement so that long-lead and critical components are identified and ordered without delay? What checks would you use to prevent omissions or miscommunication?
2. **Fabrication and assembly sequencing:** Given a multi-phase build (e.g. cutting → welding → fitting → assembly), how would you sequence fabrication and assembly tasks to minimize idle time and bottlenecks? Give a concrete example from your experience.
3. **Task management and gates:** How would you use a task system (e.g. Asana or similar) to assign, track, and gate tasks across production, procurement, and design/CAM teams? How would you handle overdue or blocked tasks?
4. **Order / Receive / Send / Back-Return:** We track job work and vendor parts with Order (O:), Receive (R:), Send (S:), and Back-Return (BR:) cycles. How would you manage this cycle and what controls would you put in place for traceability and accountability at each step?
5. **Delays and cross-functional resolution:** When a fabrication task (e.g. welding) is delayed because a part is missing, how would you coordinate with procurement and the Factory Head to resolve it and update the production schedule?
6. **Dispatch readiness and punch list:** Walk us through how you would prepare a machine for dispatch. How do you ensure all punch list items are closed, documentation is complete, and the machine meets readiness criteria before delivery?
7. **Daily/weekly coordination:** How would you run coordination between production, procurement, CAD/CAM, and the Factory Head (e.g. daily or weekly)? What key metrics or dashboards would you review to keep projects on track?
8. **Quality traceback:** If a quality issue is found during testing/trial, how would you trace the root cause back through BOM, fabrication, and assembly and implement corrective action?
9. **Job work and external vendors:** How do you manage job work that goes to external vendors (Send/Back-Return)? How do you monitor turnaround, quality, and reintegration into the main production flow?
10. **Phase handover:** How would you structure and document handover between phases (e.g. fabrication to fitting, assembly to testing) so that responsibilities are clear and rework is minimized?

**Part C — Production planning case study**  
Please read the short **case study below** (based on how we run projects at Machinecraft Umargam). Then in **200–300 words**, suggest what you would do as Plant Manager: what you’d prioritise, whom you’d involve, and what you’d do in the first 48 hours and the first week.

**Case study: PF1 project — delayed procurement and blocked fitting**

*Project:* 22014 – ALP Delhi – PF1 3520 SA (PF1 machine, 3.5 m × 2 m forming area).  
*Customer:* ALP Delhi. Committed Ex-Works Umargam dispatch date: 6 weeks from today.

*Current state (as of this week):*
- **BOM:** Frozen. Mechanical BOM, cylinders & pneumatics, and electrical sections are signed off.
- **Procurement:** Most raw material and hardware have been received. One critical item is late: **Festo plug cylinders (DSBC-100-100-PPVA)** — Order (O:) was placed 5 weeks ago; Receive (R:) was expected 2 weeks ago. Vendor has communicated a further 2-week delay. No substitute has been ordered.
- **Fabrication:** Main plates 1 & 2 are welded (W: done). Base plate and front/back body frames are in progress. Welding team will finish the remaining fabrication in about 1 week if no new delays.
- **Fitting:** Fitting (FT:) for HMI box, cross cutter, vacuum tank, and **pneumatic cylinders** cannot start until the Festo cylinders are received. Assembly and electrical cannot fully proceed without these.
- **Factory Head** is aware and has asked for a “plan A and plan B” from production. **Procurement** says they are chasing the vendor daily but have no firm date. **Sales** has not yet informed the customer of a possible slip.

*Your task:* As the Plant Manager for Umargam, how would you approach this situation? In 200–300 words, outline what you would prioritise, whom you would involve (e.g. Procurement, Factory Head, CAD, Sales), and what concrete steps you would take in the first 48 hours and in the first week. If you would ask for any additional information before deciding, say what you’d need.
