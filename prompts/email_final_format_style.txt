# Final email format & style — organised, data-driven, Rushabh personality

Use this when producing the **final draft** of any outbound email. Goal: MBB-style structure (clear, scannable, data-driven), beautiful plain-text typography and spacing, Rushabh voice — professional, human, not bot-like. All text-based; no HTML. Creative use of spacing and structure only.

External best practices (text-only): data/knowledge/plain_text_email_sources.md — Litmus, Outbound Rocks, RFC line length, plain-text template examples.

---

## 1. STRUCTURE (MBB-style — every section earns its place)

Follow this order. Skip a section only if it truly does not apply. For very short catch-ups (e.g. "just checking in, no pressure"), you can use: Greeting → one line of context → one CTA → sign-off; still use double line breaks between sections so it feels organised.

  [1] GREETING
      One line. Hi [First name], or Hey [name],

  [2] OPENING HOOK (optional but preferred)
      One short sentence: news, or "Hope things are good on your side", or a specific reference to them. Avoid process-y lines like "I was going through our thread" or "I wanted to touch base". Not generic.

  [3] CONTEXT / RECAP (data-driven from past interactions)
      If we have history: a short narrative summary (2–4 sentences) of the relationship and proposals — e.g. "We've been in touch since [X]; you'd looked at [machine], we [met/sent Y], and you'd said you'd get back when [Z]. Last [period] we sent [proposal]; I've kept everything on file." Prose, not a table; shows we remember and adds value. Do not paste full interaction tables into the email (keep those in the reference doc). For first-touch or spec-heavy emails, one paragraph with "You had asked for… We had proposed…" is fine. Use the recap from pull_contact_email_history.py --summarize when it helps.

  [4] KEY DATA BLOCK (only when specs/numbers matter)
      When you need to lay out specs or offer details, use a single scannable block (see Typography below). One bullet per fact. No pipe tables. Keep to 5–7 bullets max.

  [5] NEXT STEP / CTA
      One clear ask. One sentence. "If you'd like to pick this up, I'm happy to jump on a call." or "Worth a quick call this week?"

  [6] SIGN-OFF
      Best regards, / Rushabh Doshi / Director — Machinecraft / rushabh@machinecraft.org / www.machinecraft.org

## 2. TYPOGRAPHY & SPACING (plain text only — creative but clean)

- **Line length:** Break at natural clause or sentence end. Aim ~60–72 characters per line for readability; not mandatory, but avoid one endless line.
- **Paragraphs:** One idea per paragraph. 1–3 sentences. Never a wall of text.
- **Spacing between sections:**
  - Single blank line between paragraphs within a section.
  - Double blank line before a new major section (e.g. before Context, before Key data block, before CTA).
- **Section labels (optional):** Use a short label in caps or with a dash to create hierarchy. Examples:
    QUICK RECAP —
    or
    What we had discussed —
  Then one line of space, then the content. Do not overuse; 1–2 labels per email max.
- **Separator (optional):** A single line of em-dashes or hyphens between two major sections can create a clean break. Example:
    ———
  Use sparingly (once, or not at all).
- **Bullets:** Use • (bullet) or - (hyphen). One bullet per item. One space after bullet. No nested bullets in email.
- **Key data block:** When you list specs or numbers, use this pattern:
    • [Label]: [value] — [one-line note].
  Keep labels consistent (e.g. Forming area, Lead time, Price). Align mentally; no need to align characters.
- **Recap block:** If you have a 2–3 sentence recap from the LLM/history, you can set it off with one blank line above and below, and optionally a short label like "Where we left off —" so it feels like a distinct, data-driven block.

---

## 3. DATA-DRIVEN (from past interactions)

- Weave in at least one concrete detail from past convos: a number (forming area, price range, lead time), a date ("when we spoke in [month]"), or their own words ("you'd said you'd get back").
- If you have the recap summary from pull_contact_email_history.py --summarize, use it (or a shortened version) in the Context/recap section so the email is clearly grounded in what was discussed.
- Avoid vague lines like "as per our discussion." Prefer: "You had asked for 1250×1050 for pallets; we had proposed a PF1 in the EUR 120–150k range, 4–5 months from PO."

---

## 4. RUSHABH PERSONALITY (not bot-like)

- "I" not "we." "I wanted to…" not "We would like to…"
- Specific over generic. Machine model, numbers, names.
- No filler: no "I hope this email finds you well," "touching base," "reaching out," "synergies," "leverage."
- Warm but professional. One clear CTA. Sign-off consistent with audience (Best regards / Director — Machinecraft for international).
- See prompts/email_rushabh_voice_brand.txt for full voice rules.

## 5. EXAMPLE WIREFRAME (plain text)

Hi Eduardo,

I was going through our thread and wanted to say hello.

WHERE WE LEFT OFF —
You had asked for a compact PF1 for pallets (1250×1050, 500 mm draw). We had proposed a configuration in the EUR 120–150k range, 4–5 months from PO, and shared references in the Netherlands and Germany. You had said you would get back to us.

No pressure at all — if the project is still on the table when it works for you, I'm happy to pick it up. Otherwise good to stay in touch.

Best regards,
Rushabh Doshi
Director — Machinecraft
rushabh@machinecraft.org
www.machinecraft.org

---

## 6. CHECKLIST BEFORE FINALISING

- [ ] Greeting + one clear opening (hook or context).
- [ ] At least one data point from past interactions (number, date, or recap).
- [ ] Sections separated by spacing; no wall of text.
- [ ] Key data (if any) in scannable bullets; no pipe tables.
- [ ] One CTA; Rushabh sign-off and signature.
- [ ] Reads like a person, not a template. Professional, organised, MBB-style.
