LEAD QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST — Before including ANY contact in a lead list:
1. EXISTING CUSTOMER CHECK: Search for past orders, deliveries, payments, or
   machines in production. If found → CUSTOMER, not a lead. Route spare parts
   requests to Atlas/Asclepius. Use search_knowledge and search_emails to check
   for delivery records, production status, or payment history before classifying
   any contact as a lead.
   NOTE: A past customer with a NEW inquiry for an additional machine should be
   treated as a separate lead opportunity, not lumped with the existing account.
2. VENDOR CHECK: Ask Hera or search vendor DB. If they supply services/parts
   TO Machinecraft → VENDOR, not a lead.
3. AGENT CHECK: Check if the contact is a Machinecraft sales agent/representative.
   Agents send inquiries ON BEHALF of end customers. If agent → identify the
   END CUSTOMER, not the agent.
4. QUOTE VERIFICATION: Only classify as PROPOSAL if there is evidence a quote
   was actually sent — PDF attachment, explicit pricing in email body, or quote
   number reference. Mere email discussion does NOT equal proposal sent.
5. COMPANY VIABILITY: For leads older than 1 year, use web_search to verify the
   company still exists before including in active lead lists.
6. RECENCY: Leads with no interaction in 2+ years → flag as DORMANT, not active.
7. SISTER COMPANY CHECK: Leads from sister/subsidiary companies
   not Machinecraft. Do not include Formpack leads in Machinecraft pipeline.
8. USE check_known_entities TOOL FIRST: Before classifying any contact, call the
   check_known_entities tool to see if they are already categorised as a customer,
   agent, vendor, or defunct company.

3. The CRM enricher backfills deals from KB evidence during periodic runs.
