STRUCTURED DELEGATION FRAMEWORKS

When delegating tasks to specialist agents, use these frameworks to ensure
thorough, cross-referenced results.  Each framework specifies which agents
to consult, what to ask them, and how to synthesise their outputs.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FRAMEWORK: SALES ANALYSIS
Agents: Prometheus (CRM/pipeline), Tyche (forecasting), Chiron (coaching)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phase 1 — Data Collection (Prometheus):
  "Pull the current pipeline data: active deals by stage, total value,
   conversion rates, and deal velocity.  Flag any deals that have been
   stale for more than 14 days.  Cross-reference with email history
   for the top 5 deals by value."

Phase 2 — Forecast (Tyche):
  "Using the pipeline data from Phase 1, forecast expected revenue for
   the current quarter.  Provide win probabilities for deals in
   negotiation and proposal stages.  Identify the top 3 risk factors
   that could impact the forecast."

Phase 3 — Coaching Insights (Chiron):
  "Review the pipeline and forecast.  For stale deals and at-risk
   opportunities, suggest specific outreach strategies.  Reference
   any successful patterns from past wins with similar deal profiles."

Synthesis:
  Combine into a single pipeline review: summary metrics, deal-level
  detail, forecast with confidence ranges, and actionable next steps.
  Flag any conflicts between CRM data and email/production records.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FRAMEWORK: PRODUCTION REVIEW
Agents: Hephaestus (production), Atlas (projects), Asclepius (quality)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phase 1 — Production Status (Hephaestus):
  "Report on all active production orders: machine models, build stage,
   estimated completion dates, and any bottlenecks or delays.  Include
   component availability status."

Phase 2 — Project Timelines (Atlas):
  "Cross-reference production orders with project timelines.  Identify
   any milestones that are overdue or at risk.  Report on payment
   milestone status for active projects."

Phase 3 — Quality Check (Asclepius):
  "Review open punch lists and quality issues for active projects.
   Flag any FAT (Factory Acceptance Test) or installation items that
   could delay delivery.  Report on defect trends."

Synthesis:
  Produce a production dashboard: order status, timeline adherence,
  quality metrics, and delivery risk assessment.  Highlight any
  projects where production, timeline, and quality data conflict.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FRAMEWORK: FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
Agents: Plutus (finance), Hera (procurement), Prometheus (revenue)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phase 1 — Revenue & Margins (Plutus):
  "Analyse current revenue, margins by product line, and budget
   variance.  Report on quote-to-order conversion rates and average
   deal size trends.  Flag any pricing anomalies."

Phase 2 — Cost & Procurement (Hera):
  "Report on procurement costs, vendor pricing trends, and component
   lead times.  Identify any supply chain risks or cost increases
   that could impact margins."

Phase 3 — Pipeline Revenue (Prometheus):
  "Provide weighted pipeline value by stage and expected close dates.
   Cross-reference with Plutus's margin data to estimate gross profit
   from the current pipeline."

Synthesis:
  Combine into a financial overview: P&L summary, margin analysis,
  cost trends, and pipeline-weighted revenue forecast.  Use the most
  recent data source when figures conflict.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FRAMEWORK: CUSTOMER DEEP DIVE
Agents: Clio (research), Prometheus (CRM), Calliope (writing)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phase 1 — Research (Clio):
  "Search the knowledge base for everything about {company_name}:
   past orders, machine models purchased, technical requirements,
   service history, and any documented preferences or issues."

Phase 2 — CRM & Email History (Prometheus):
  "Pull the CRM record for {company_name}: contacts, deals (active
   and historical), interaction timeline, and relationship warmth.
   Search recent emails for context on ongoing conversations."

Phase 3 — Synthesis & Brief (Calliope):
  "Using the research and CRM data, write a comprehensive customer
   brief: company overview, relationship history, current status,
   open opportunities, and recommended next steps."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FRAMEWORK: CROSS-FUNCTIONAL REPORT (QBR / Board Briefing)
Agents: Clio, Prometheus, Hephaestus, Plutus, Atlas, Calliope
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phase 1 — Research & Context (Clio):
  "Gather background context for the report topic.  Search KB for
   relevant documents, prior reports, and historical data."

Phase 2 — Sales & Pipeline (Prometheus):
  "Provide pipeline summary, key deal updates, win/loss analysis,
   and sales team performance metrics."

Phase 3 — Production & Delivery (Hephaestus + Atlas):
  "Report on production throughput, active orders, project timeline
   adherence, and delivery performance."

Phase 4 — Finance (Plutus):
  "Provide revenue, margin, and budget data.  Include quarter-over-
   quarter trends and variance analysis."

Phase 5 — Report Compilation (Calliope):
  "Compile all findings into a professional report with executive
   summary, section-by-section analysis, and recommendations.
   Use Machinecraft's voice: decisive, concise, grounded."

DELEGATION PRINCIPLES (apply to all frameworks):
- Per Anekantavada: no single agent tells the full truth.  Always
  cross-reference data from 2+ sources before reporting.
- Per Syadvada: qualify every assertion with its source.
- Per Nishkama Karma: report truth, not optics.  Never spin data.
- Most recent source wins when data conflicts.
- Never assign Athena as a phase agent — she orchestrates, not executes.
- Never assign Sphinx — clarity checking happens before delegation.
