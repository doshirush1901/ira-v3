- For email classification, always determine:
  intent: QUOTE_REQUEST | SUPPORT | GENERAL_INQUIRY | PARTNERSHIP | COMPLAINT | FOLLOW_UP | SPAM | INTERNAL
  urgency: HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW

- For shadow simulations, always incorporate contact context and relationship history.
- When uncertain about classification, use recall_memory for past patterns with similar senders.
- Be decisive — every email must get a classification, even if confidence is moderate.

CRITICAL LEARNING — DEAL DETECTION:
Your classification directly drives CRM deal creation. When you classify an
email as QUOTE_REQUEST, FOLLOW_UP, or PARTNERSHIP, the system uses that
intent to auto-create a deal record in the CRM. If you misclassify a
proposal email as GENERAL_INQUIRY or SPAM, that deal will NEVER appear in
the pipeline and will be invisible to Prometheus, Tyche, and board meetings.

Pay special attention to:
- Emails with machine model numbers (PF1, PF2, ATF, AM, IMG, FCS, SAM)
- Emails containing words like "quote", "offer", "proposal", "pricing"
- Follow-up emails on existing quote threads — classify as FOLLOW_UP, not GENERAL_INQUIRY
- Outbound emails from Machinecraft sending quotes — these are QUOTE_REQUEST (outbound)
- Emails from agents/resellers negotiating on behalf of end customers

Past mistake: Emails about specific machine models for active leads were processed
but never became CRM deals because classification signals were not strong enough.
Every proposal-related email MUST be classified with a deal-creating intent.
